# Olist Customer Retention & Reactivation Segmentation

## Overview

This project analyzes customer purchasing behavior using the Olist Brazilian e-commerce dataset. The goal is to identify different customer segments based on purchase recency, order frequency, and lifetime spending.

The analysis creates a segmentation framework that helps identify high-value customers, customers at risk of churn, and customers who have become inactive.

---

## Business Problem

E-commerce companies need to understand which customers generate the most value and whether those customers remain engaged with the platform.

By analyzing customer purchase history, businesses can:

- Identify **high-value active customers**
- Detect **high-value customers who are at risk of churn**
- Understand the distribution of revenue across different customer segments
- Prioritize retention and reactivation strategies

---

## Dataset

This analysis uses the **Olist Brazilian E-commerce Dataset**, which contains order and payment data from an online marketplace.

Key tables used:

- `orders` – order level information including customer ID, order status, and purchase timestamps
- `order_payments` – payment values associated with each order

---

## Methodology

The SQL analysis builds customer-level metrics using a series of Common Table Expressions (CTEs).

### 1. Order Cleaning
Filter the dataset to include only **delivered orders** with valid purchase timestamps.

### 2. Revenue Calculation
Aggregate order payments to calculate total revenue per order.

### 3. Customer Rollup
Create customer-level metrics:

- Lifetime number of orders
- Lifetime spending
- Last purchase date

### 4. Recency Calculation
Calculate the number of days since each customer’s last purchase.

### 5. Customer Segmentation
Customers are categorized using a **rule-based segmentation model** based on:

- Lifetime spending
- Purchase frequency
- Purchase recency

Segments include:

- **VIP Active**
- **VIP At-Risk**
- **Loyal Active**
- **Loyal At-Risk**
- **Repeat Recent**
- **Repeat Lapsed**
- **New**
- **One-time Lapsed**

### 6. Segment Performance Summary
The final query aggregates results to measure:

- Number of customers per segment
- Average orders per customer
- Average spending
- Total revenue generated by each segment
- Revenue share contribution

---

## SQL Concepts Demonstrated

- Common Table Expressions (CTEs)
- Aggregation (`SUM`, `COUNT`, `AVG`)

- Customer-level metric construction

## Output

The final query produces a **segment-level performance table** showing:

- customer_segment
- customers
- average orders
- average spend
- total revenue
- revenue share

This allows analysts to quickly identify which customer segments drive the most revenue and where retention efforts should be focused.
